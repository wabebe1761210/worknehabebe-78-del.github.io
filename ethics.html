IT Ethics

IT ethics means using technology in a responsible, honest, and respectful way. People who work in technology should think about how their work affects users, communities, and society.

Privacy and Security

IT professionals should protect users' private information. They should not collect or share personal data without permission. They should also build secure systems to help prevent hacking, identity theft, and misuse of data.

Fairness and Responsibility

Developers and data scientists should try to create technology that is fair and does not harm people. For example, algorithms should be tested carefully so they do not create unfair results or bias against certain groups.

Professional Behavior

IT workers should be honest about their skills, follow laws and rules, and respect the people who use their systems. Good technology should help people, not take advantage of them.

Reference

Association for Computing Machinery. (2018). ACM Code of Ethics and Professional Conduct. https://www.acm.org/code-of-ethics
